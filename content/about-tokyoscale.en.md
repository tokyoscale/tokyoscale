TokyoScale is headquartered in Tokyo. We work with established Japanese organizations that carry deep institutional knowledge—and also face succession pressure, labor shortages, and uneven digitization.

## What we believe

Economies shift when workflows become safer and calmer—when routine information moves cleanly, repetition falls away, teams stop paying a silent “complexity tax,” and modernization helps people—not only margins.

TokyoScale is **not built as an AI agency billboard**. Engineering matters: reliable systems, clear ownership, repeatable delivery. Still, software is seldom the entire answer. Adoption, sequencing, governance, staffing, procurement, robotics, sensing, incentives, regulation, geography, unions, lineage of craft—all of these travel together.

We prefer honest trade‑offs stated early over polished stories that crumble on the weekly operations call.

## How we behave

**Partnership beats promises.** We favor structures—and incentives—that align when timelines stretch and surprises surface mid‑migration.

**Respect lineage competence.** Institutional memory is scarce. Tools should amplify people who carry it.

**Automation with safeguards.** Humans need override paths, escalation clarity, observable failure boundaries—especially where physical risk or vulnerable residents are involved.

**Human consequences first.** In elderly care, “efficiency” that quietly pushes burden toward residents or frontline staff fails our test—even when spreadsheets look flattering.

TokyoScale is young; humility suits us. We are building calmly and carefully—toward futures we intend to inhabit ourselves.
